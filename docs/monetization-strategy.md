## 1. Getting More Eyes on Your Course

### Udemy's Internal Search Algorithm

Udemy's algorithm evaluates courses on four core factors with the following approximate weights:

- **Completion Rate (40%)** — The single most important factor. Courses that students actually finish rank dramatically higher. Average Udemy completion is only ~10%, so even modest improvements here give you a major edge.
- **Average Rating (30%)** — Both the star rating and the recency of reviews matter. Reviews from the last 90 days carry more weight. For subcategory rankings specifically, review count is the dominant factor.
- **Enrollment Velocity (20%)** — The speed at which new students enroll. A burst of 50 enrollments in a week signals more relevance than 50 spread over a year. This is why launch momentum matters so much.
- **Instructor Responsiveness (10%)** — Q&A response activity, engagement in discussion forums, and course update frequency all contribute here.

Additional ranking signals include keyword relevance in title/subtitle/description, wishlisting activity, and watch-through percentage per lecture. The algorithm updates daily.

### SEO for Udemy Courses

**Title optimization** is the highest-leverage SEO activity on Udemy. Place your primary keyword at the very beginning of your title — this is the single most common mistake new instructors make. Keep your keyword focus to 1-2 word terms; Udemy's search does not support long-tail queries the way Google does. There is no long-tail on Udemy search.

For **subtitle optimization**, use all 120 characters available. Include at least 3 secondary keywords woven naturally alongside benefit statements. Think of it as ad copy, not a keyword dump.

In your **course description**, include primary and secondary keywords naturally throughout. Search for your target keyword on Udemy first to gauge competition — if thousands of results come back, consider targeting a less competitive variation.

For **tags**, research what learners actually search for by using Udemy's search autocomplete dropdown. Type your topic and note every suggestion — these reflect real student search behavior and are more valuable than external keyword tools.

### External Traffic Strategies

**YouTube** is the highest-impact external channel. Create tutorials related to your course topic, optimized with proven title formulas. Offer genuine value in the free video, then direct viewers to your paid course for deeper learning. Guest appearances on established channels in your niche can accelerate growth.

**Blog content and guest posts** on reputable sites in your niche drive long-tail organic traffic. Include natural mentions of your course with referral links.

**Social media** (LinkedIn for professional/tech courses, Twitter/X for developer audiences, Instagram for broader topics) works best when you share genuine expertise rather than promotional content. Engage in relevant communities on Quora, Reddit, and Medium.

**Email list building** delivers the highest ROI of any channel. Tools like ConvertKit or MailerLite let you set up a list quickly. Offer a free resource (PDF guide, cheat sheet, mini-course) to capture emails. Start building your list 3-6 months before launch.

**Webinars** can be powerful — hosts report that 200+ attendee webinars lead to significant signups, especially when combined with limited-time coupon codes.

### Udemy Deals and Promotions

When you register as a premium instructor, you are **opted into the Udemy Deals program by default**. To manage this, log into your instructor account, click "Manage Promotional Agreements," scroll to "Udemy Deals Program," and make your selection.

Being opted in does not guarantee inclusion — Udemy selects courses based on topic relevance, enrollment history, conversion rates, ratings, and student wishlisting activity. Subscribe to promotional emails in your notification settings to track when your course appears in promotions.

Opting out means losing participation in Udemy's external promotion channels (search ads, social media retargeting, homepage features). It takes time for your course to fully disappear from external channels after opting out.

### Cross-Promotion with Other Instructors

Legitimate collaboration is encouraged: referrals, affiliate partnerships, interviews, co-created content, and cross-promotions with complementary (not competing) instructors. Leveraging another instructor's credibility through "warm introductions" to their audience yields higher conversion than cold promotion.

However, Udemy **prohibits** using co-instructor status primarily to exploit marketing tools or share student bases. If you want to promote each other's courses, use the Affiliate Program instead of co-instructor arrangements.

### Udemy's Affiliate Program

The affiliate program pays a **base commission of 10%** on valid sales, with performance-based increases to 15-20% for top performers. The cookie window is 7 days — the customer must purchase within 7 days of clicking your affiliate link. Free courses, unapproved coupon purchases, and subscription purchases are excluded from commissions. Some recent sources report rates as high as 17% per sale.

### Organic Traffic vs. Instructor-Driven Traffic

When a student purchases using your coupon or referral link, the sale is immediately attributed to you as instructor-driven. When no coupon or link is used, the sale is temporarily classified as "Udemy-driven" for 72 hours, then resolves to specific channels (organic search, ads, affiliates, etc.).

### Revenue Split

This is the single most important number to internalize:

- **Instructor-driven traffic (your coupon/referral link):** You keep **97%**, Udemy keeps 3%
- **Organic/marketplace traffic (Udemy drives the sale):** You receive **37%**, Udemy keeps 63%
- **Udemy Business subscriptions:** Revenue pool divided by minutes consumed — currently **17.5%** of subscription revenue goes to the instructor pool (January 2025), dropping to **15%** in January 2026

A student acquired through your own marketing is worth **2.6x more** than one from Udemy's organic marketplace. This makes external marketing not just beneficial but essential for serious revenue.

## 2. What Gets the Best Ratings

### Course Structure Best Practices

**Optimal lecture length** is 2-6 minutes according to Udemy's own research, with a practical sweet spot of 3-8 minutes. Engagement data shows a decline between minutes 1-3, a level period from 6-12 minutes, then steady decline after 12 minutes. Never exceed 12 minutes per lecture unless the content absolutely demands it.

**Total course length** varies by topic. The industry average across all Udemy courses is 3 hours; the top 10,000 courses average 5 hours. The optimal range is 5-10 hours for most topics. Recommended lengths by category:

- Technology/Programming: 4-10 hours
- Business/Marketing: 4-10 hours
- Niche/sub-topic courses: ~1 hour
- Free courses: Maximum 2 hours

Minimum requirements are 30 minutes of video and 5 lectures. Udemy supports up to 1,400 curriculum items per course with 800 published at once.

**Section organization** should follow one learning objective per section, building progressively. Each section typically contains 5-15 minutes of content spread across multiple short lectures.

### Video and Audio Quality Standards

**Video**: Minimum 720p resolution (1080p recommended), 16:9 aspect ratio, horizontal orientation. HD quality is required.

**Audio**: Clear and non-distracting, output from both stereo channels, consistent volume levels. Common quality killers include distortion, background hiss, volume inconsistencies, and unsynchronized audio. Edit out "ums," long pauses, and verbal mistakes. Demonstrate genuine enthusiasm — instructor energy is directly correlated with student satisfaction.

### Engagement Tactics

Include at least one learning activity per section — a quiz, exercise, or project. Place your first activity within the first few lectures to establish engagement patterns early.

**Quiz design**: Questions should require genuine comprehension, not just recall. Provide detailed explanations for correct answers and explain how each activity connects to course content.

**Types of engagement activities**: quizzes (multiple choice for reinforcement), assignments (reflections, case studies with peer/instructor review), coding exercises (hands-on practice), practice tests (full assessments), and projects (real-world application). Practice activities are not technically required but are strongly recommended by Udemy and heavily correlated with higher ratings.

### Q&A Response Time

Udemy does not publish a specific algorithm penalty for slow Q&A response times, but it is part of broader quality assessment. Students report significantly higher satisfaction when instructors actively answer questions and spark discussion. Udemy's AI analyzes all learner input (reviews, Q&As, fast feedback) daily, flagging content quality signals. Best practice is to respond within 24-48 hours.

### Course Updates and Freshness

Udemy calculates an "average content age" metric based on the age of each curriculum item. This metric flags potentially outdated content, with different thresholds by subject domain. Some topics are evergreen; AI/tech courses are not.

Udemy's quality assessment is expanding to detect low-quality AI-generated content, audio problems, and readability issues. Many top-performing courses are updated several times per year. Regular updates signal active maintenance and directly improve both ranking and student trust.

### Common Reasons for Low Ratings

The most frequent causes of 1-3 star reviews: poor pacing (especially toward course end), rushed or unclear explanations, buggy or incomplete code without context, subtitle errors, lack of depth for advanced learners, low instructor engagement, mismatched expectations (content, teaching style, or language), outdated content, and ignoring Q&A questions.

Prevention strategy: deliver a strong first impression, maintain consistent pacing throughout, test all code and explain thoroughly, provide accurate subtitles, engage actively in Q&A, respond constructively to negative reviews, and keep content updated. Treat every review as a feedback loop for improvement.

### The First 30 Minutes (and the Refund Window)

Udemy's refund policy is a **30-day window** from purchase (not 30 minutes). Students can request refunds within 30 days provided they haven't consumed a "notable amount" of the course. Multiple refunds for the same course or excessive refund patterns may be denied. Udemy holds instructor payments for 30 days to account for this.

Despite the 30-day window, the **first impression is critical**. Students are prompted to leave a star rating after watching approximately 12-15 minutes of content. This means your opening lectures need to deliver exceptional value immediately — they determine both your early ratings and whether students continue or refund.

## 3. Naming Best Practices

### Title Formulas That Work

Three proven patterns dominate bestselling courses:

**Pattern 1 — Topic + Journey**: "[Topic]: [Action] from [Beginner] to [Advanced]"
Examples: "Microsoft Excel: Master from Beginner to Advanced" or "Learn Advanced Excel Formulas taught by Googler"

**Pattern 2 — Topic + Year + Outcome**: "[Topic] [Year]: [Skill] & [Outcome]"
Examples: "YouTube Mastery Course with AI 2026: Rank, Grow & Monetize" or "Master SEO 2026: From Beginner to Pro with AI Techniques"

**Pattern 3 — Topic + Complete Path**: "[Topic]: Complete [Level] to [Level] [Focus Area]"
Example: "EXCEL: Master Microsoft Excel from Basics to Advanced Excel"

Adding the current year (2025/2026) signals freshness and is especially effective for tech courses. All top performers use outcome-oriented language: "Master," "Learn," "Build," "Complete."

### Subtitle Optimization

The subtitle has a **120-character limit** and should include 3-4 key topic areas covered, at least 3 secondary keywords, audience level (beginner/intermediate/advanced), and tools or outcomes students will achieve. Write it as benefit-focused ad copy, not a keyword list.

### Character Limits

- **Title**: 60 characters maximum (enforced)
- **Subtitle**: 120 characters maximum (enforced)

These are hard limits. Plan your titles accordingly — every character counts.

### Keyword Research for Udemy Titles

**Udemy's Marketplace Insights** is the most valuable built-in tool — it lets you search topics and identify Udemy-specific keyword frequency.

The single best free research method: type your topic into Udemy's search box and note every autocomplete suggestion. These reflect actual student search behavior on the platform and are more actionable than any external tool.

External tools for supplementary research: Google Keyword Planner (free), Ahrefs Keyword Explorer, SEMrush, and Long Tail Pro.

Research workflow: (1) Start with your main keyword in Udemy search, (2) capture all autocomplete suggestions, (3) check competitor course titles for secondary keywords, (4) cross-reference with external tools for volume data, (5) verify monthly search volume before finalizing.

### Top-Performing Titles in Tech/AI (March 2026)

Current bestsellers to study:

- "LLM Engineering: Master AI, LLMs & Agents"
- "The AI Engineer Course 2025: Complete AI Engineer Bootcamp" (204,000+ students, 4.7 stars)
- "The Complete Prompt Engineering for AI Bootcamp (2025)" (135,967 reviews)
- "Artificial Intelligence A-Z 2026: Learn How To Build An AI"
- "Full AI Course 2026: ChatGPT, Gemini, Midjourney, Firefly" (21,748+ students)
- "AI for Business Leaders" (8,800+ students, Bestseller badge)

Common patterns: year included, specific skill focus, outcome-oriented language, technical credibility.

### A/B Testing Titles

Udemy **does not natively support A/B testing** for course titles. Workaround: launch with your best research-backed title, monitor analytics for 2-4 weeks (page views, conversion rate, click-through rate, wishlist adds), then request a title change if performance is below target. Track metrics before and after to measure impact. Invest in getting the title right pre-launch rather than relying on post-launch iteration.

## 4. Pricing Strategy

### Udemy's Pricing Tiers

Udemy uses a fixed tier structure with 37 tiers:

- **Minimum (Tier 1)**: $19.99
- **Maximum (Tier 37)**: $199.99
- **Increment**: ~$5 between tiers
- **All prices end in .99**

Udemy also maintains a global price tier matrix adjusted for different currencies. Instructor coupons can be set from $0 (free) to 99% off, but standard marketplace prices must align with the tier structure.

### Free vs. Paid — When to Use Each

**Free courses** get 1.8x more page views than paid courses and build your student base quickly. However, they offer no certificate, limited features (no Q&A, no direct messaging), and now include ads for students. Use free courses as lead generation for paid courses, for building instructor credibility, for testing content before paid launch, or for establishing initial reviews faster.

**Paid courses** offer lifetime access, higher perceived value, full platform features, and revenue potential. Use paid when you have proven content, established credentials, or a complementary free course driving traffic.

**Hybrid strategy**: Launch a free introductory course to gather reviews and build credibility, then funnel students to paid advanced/specialized courses.

### Coupon Strategy

**Instructor coupons** earn you **97% revenue** per sale. You can create up to 3 coupons per course per month, with a bulk tool for scheduling multiple coupons. Distribute via social media, email, announcements, and direct messages. You control the discount percentage, duration, and redemption limits.

**Udemy Deals** earn you **37% revenue** but reach Udemy's full marketing channels (email, app, homepage). Udemy determines the discount level and timing — you have no control. New courses get a 14-day launch protection period where they are not auto-discounted.

**Optimal approach**: Use instructor coupons first for maximum ROI on your promotional efforts, then use Udemy Deals as a supplementary volume play.

### Revenue Share Model

| Channel | Instructor Share | Udemy Share |
|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| Instructor Coupon/Referral | 97% | 3% |
| Organic Marketplace | 37% | 63% |
| Udemy Business (2025) | 17.5% pool | 82.5% |
| Udemy Business (2026) | 15% pool | 85% |

### Pricing Psychology

Research-backed price point strategies:

- **$19.99**: Entry point, highest volume, lowest perceived value
- **$49.99-$74.99**: Sweet spot for skill-focused courses
- **$99.99-$149.99**: Specialized or advanced courses
- **$199.99**: Comprehensive bootcamp-style courses

Learners use price as a quality signal — a $99.99 course with strong credentials signals more value than $19.99 on the same topic. The ".99" ending is the standard psychological anchor across the platform.

### Bundling and Course Series

Udemy supports bundles of 2-3 published courses. Create bundles along natural learning paths: Beginner to Advanced, Fundamentals plus Specializations, or tool-based series. Price bundles at roughly 15-20% discount vs. individual prices (e.g., two $49.99 courses bundled at $79.99). Bundles appear on complementary course landing pages, increase lifetime student value, and improve completion rates.

## 5. Launch Strategy

### Pre-Launch Checklist

**Content Requirements:**
- Minimum 30 minutes of video content
- Minimum 5 lectures
- HD video quality (720p minimum, 1080p recommended)
- Clear stereo audio, synced to video
- High-quality course image meeting Udemy standards
- Varied lecture formats (not all talking head or all screencast)

**Landing Page Content:**
- Title: 60 characters or less with primary keyword first
- Subtitle: 120 characters with secondary keywords and benefits
- Course description with clear benefits, learning outcomes, and teaching approach
- 4-10 specific, measurable learning objectives
- Clear target audience definition
- Correct primary category selected
- Pricing set using tier structure

**AI Content Disclosure (New 2025):** If you used generative AI in course creation, you must explicitly disclose this in your course description. AI-generated courses without instructor expertise are flagged for quality review.

### Soft Launch vs. Hard Launch

**Soft launch** (recommended first): Target your limited warm audience (email list, social followers) with significant discounts for 7-14 days. Goal is to gather feedback, identify issues, and collect 4-10 initial reviews before broader promotion. Lower risk and cost.

**Hard launch** (phase 2): Coordinate all channels simultaneously — social media, email, partnerships. Drive maximum traffic in the first 48 hours to trigger enrollment velocity signals in Udemy's algorithm. Higher impact but higher risk without initial reviews.

**Best approach**: Soft launch for 7-14 days to collect reviews, then hard launch with social proof. Use Udemy's 14-day launch protection period (no auto-discounting) to maximize your promotional efforts.

### Getting Initial Reviews

Students receive a star rating prompt after watching **12-15 minutes** of content. This makes your opening lectures the most important content in your entire course from a review perspective.

Strategies for first reviews: request feedback naturally in your introduction video, include review requests in your auto-welcome email, send personal messages asking for honest reviews, use announcement emails, have knowledgeable colleagues watch and leave credible reviews mentioning their expertise, and place strategic review requests between early lectures.

Target 4-10 reviews in your first 1-2 weeks. Reviews dramatically improve search visibility, recommendation eligibility, and email marketing inclusion.

### Promotional Pricing for Launch

Udemy's **14-day launch protection** (since September 2023) means your new paid course will not be automatically discounted for 14 days. Use this window strategically:

**Days 1-14**: Set your list price at $49.99-$74.99 to establish perceived value. Create instructor coupons at 25-40% off for your warm audience. Distribute via email and social with urgency messaging ("limited-time launch pricing").

**Days 15-60**: Phase 2 — continue instructor coupons plus opt into Udemy Deals for broader reach.

**Day 60+**: Ongoing coupon strategy combined with seasonal sales (Black Friday/Cyber Monday offers discounts up to 95% off and is the deepest discount event of the year).

### Social Proof Building

Primary tactics: credible expert reviews from professionals in your field, student testimonials featured in announcements, prominent display of star ratings and review count in all marketing, enrollment count callouts ("Join 500+ students"), and instructor credential/experience highlights.

In all email marketing, lead with social proof: rating, review highlights, enrollment numbers. On your course page, display reviews prominently, include testimonial quotes in description, and highlight your background.

### Email Marketing Sequence

**Pre-launch (14 days before):** Announcement email ("Coming Soon" with benefits), benefit-focused email (Day 4), social proof email with expert endorsements (Day 7), urgency email with early-bird deadline (Day 10).

**Launch week:** Launch day email with coupon, 24-hour follow-up reinforcing offer, Day 3 sharing early student feedback, Day 5 last call with enrollment numbers, Day 7 transition email ("Launch pricing ending soon").

**Post-launch:** Email broader audience leading with reviews and social proof, continue coupon distribution, create urgency around seasonal events.

Keep subject lines under 50 characters, lead with benefits, include social proof, use a clear call-to-action with coupon link, and optimize for mobile.

## 6. Content Strategy

### What Makes a Course "Bestseller"

To earn the Bestseller badge, a course must meet **both** criteria: highest recent sales within its specific category + primary topic combination (e.g., Python in Development), **and** a minimum 4.2 average rating.

Udemy displays only one badge per course, in priority order: Bestseller > Highest Rated > Hot and New (expires after 60 days) > New. The Highest Rated badge requires a high concentration of 5-star reviews — volume matters (1,000 reviews at 4.8 stars beats 10 reviews at 4.9 stars) and no more than 5% should be 1-star ratings.

### Optimal Course Length by Topic

| Category | Recommended Length |
|----------|-------------------|
| Technology/Programming | 4-10 hours |
| Business/Marketing | 4-10 hours |
| Niche/Sub-topic | ~1 hour |
| General sweet spot | 5-10 hours |
| Minimum viable | 4-6 hours core content |
| Free courses | Max 2 hours |

Key principle: length should match audience motivation. Problem-solvers want shorter, focused courses; concept-learners benefit from deeper, longer courses. A dense 4-hour course outperforms a padded 10-hour course every time.

### Structuring for Maximum Completion Rate

With an average completion rate of only ~10% across Udemy, even modest improvements here significantly boost your algorithm ranking (completion is weighted at 40%).

**Short, focused lectures**: Single-task lectures (2-6 minutes) allow quick reference and easy revisiting. Students feel progress with each completed lecture.

**Project-based learning**: Present the final project upfront as a motivating goal. Build step-by-step toward completion. Each stage provides a sense of accomplishment.

**Clear progression**: Include definitive milestones throughout. Provide notes on reasoning and resources at each step. Students should always know where they are in the journey.

**Dense, valuable content**: Avoid padding with repetition or over-explanation. A focused 2-hour course with real value gets better completion than 10 hours of filler.

**Student communication**: Send a welcome email with login info, expectations, suggested schedule, and support information. This addresses the two main dropout reasons: getting busy and losing motivation.

### Bonus Content and Resources

Bonus lectures must be labeled "Bonus Lecture" and cannot be referenced on your course landing page. They can include social media follow requests, email sign-ups, and additional practice materials.

Valuable resources that boost completion and satisfaction: downloadable PDFs, templates, code files, practice worksheets, case studies, and additional reading lists. Provide all materials needed for each activity. Quality resources justify higher pricing and drive better reviews.

### Certificate of Completion

Only paid, approved courses offer certificates. Udemy is not an accredited institution, so certificates demonstrate skill completion but cannot be used for formal accreditation. Students can add them to LinkedIn. Setting certificate expiration dates can nudge learners to revisit or purchase follow-up courses. While no specific data links certificates to sales conversions, they are a standard expectation for paid courses.

## 7. Tech/AI Course Specifics

### Certification Prep Courses That Perform Well

Cloud certifications dominate the top-earning tech courses: **AWS certifications** (Cloud Practitioner through ML Specialist) are the market leader, followed by **Azure** and **Google Cloud** (GCP Professional Cloud Architect has 407,000+ subscriptions). CompTIA certifications also perform well.

Development courses average **$2,339/year** per instructor, IT & Software averages **$1,494/year** — both significantly above the platform average and far above lifestyle ($300/year) or music ($350/year) categories.

### Anthropic/Claude — Demand and Competition

Claude-specific courses exist on Udemy but are **significantly underrepresented** compared to ChatGPT/OpenAI courses. A review of 20+ Claude Code courses found variable quality — many appear created for quick returns without deep expertise.

Current Claude courses on Udemy include:
- "Claude Code: Building Faster with AI, from Prototype to Prod" (Frank Kane, former Amazon senior engineer, 1M+ learner following)
- "Claude Pro Mastery: AI for Business, Marketing & Automation"
- "Claude AI: The AI Assistant You'll Actually Use"
- "Complete Claude, Claude Code & AI for Work Productivity"

**Market assessment**: This represents a genuine gap. Claude tools are advancing rapidly (Claude Code, MCP, extended context windows), but course coverage lags far behind ChatGPT. There is a first-mover advantage opportunity for comprehensive, high-quality Claude courses — especially around Claude Code, the Agent SDK, and MCP integration.

### Differentiating from Free Content

The key differentiators that justify paid courses over YouTube/docs: structured curriculum with progressive learning paths (YouTube lacks this), hands-on labs and projects embedded in course flow, community support with instructor Q&A (YouTube rarely offers this), lifetime access vs. algorithm-dependent discoverability, and interactive features (quizzes, coding exercises, practice tests).

Courses that build real, production-grade systems dramatically outperform tutorial-style content. The 2026 market expects engineers who can build real AI systems, deploy them to production, reason about tradeoffs, and explain decisions clearly — not tutorial followers.

### Hands-On Labs and Projects

In-course labs increase knowledge retention by up to 75% according to some studies. Courses featuring 40+ real projects significantly outperform tutorial-heavy courses. Udemy offers three lab types: in-course labs (guided tasks in pre-configured environments), project-based labs (standalone real-world projects), and Udemy Business Labs (for B2B customers).

Best practice for 2026: shift from teaching theory to shipping production code. Build real systems throughout the course.

### Keeping AI Courses Current

AI courses require the most frequent updates of any category. Top-performing AI courses update monthly. Last update date is a key factor learners check when evaluating a course — outdated AI content directly impacts future earnings and ranking.

Update triggers for AI courses: new model releases, API changes, framework updates, new capabilities (like tool use, vision, etc.), and competitive landscape shifts. Commit to a monthly update cadence at minimum.

## 8. 2025-2026 Policy Changes

### Revenue Share Reduction

Udemy Business subscription revenue share for instructors: 20% (2024) → 17.5% (2025) → **15% (2026)**. This is a significant and ongoing trend. Instructor earnings peaked at $209.5M in 2023 and have been declining with reduced payouts.

### Subscription Pivot

Subscription revenue now represents **74% of total Udemy revenue**. Udemy is "intentionally reducing" single-course sales to push subscriptions. Q3 2025 total revenue: $195.7M ($132.8M B2B, $62.9M B2C). Consumer B2C revenue is declining while enterprise B2B is growing. This makes driving your own traffic (97% share on direct sales) more important than ever.

### Payout Threshold

Effective September 1, 2025: Minimum **$25 USD balance** required before payment processing. Below this threshold, payments are held until accumulation.

### GenAI Policy and Disclosure

Courses using generative AI in creation must disclose usage. Instructors were automatically enrolled in Udemy's GenAI program (opt-out deadline was August 2025). Udemy states it does not use learner/instructor data to train GenAI models. Opted-out content is excluded from Udemy's GenAI tools.

### Free Course Changes

Udemy introduced ads on free courses in 170 countries (July 2025). Instructors do not share any revenue from these ads.

### New Instructor Programs

**Content Innovation Fund**: $2.5 million to support next-generation learning formats (subscriptions, short-form content, AI-driven micro-learning).

**Instructor Subscriptions** (new): Enables recurring revenue through live sessions, short-form content, and community interaction.

**Instructor Partner Program**: Requires 4.4+ average rating and 3+ courses in Udemy Business collection. Benefits include priority support, exclusive events with Udemy leadership, priority marketing consideration, and expanded technical support.

### Udemy Business (B2B) Inclusion

Apply through the Premium Instructor Application. Maintain high ratings and course quality. Udemy prioritizes fully localized content from fluent/native speakers. Benefits include access to enterprise audiences and additional revenue streams, though the revenue share (15-17.5%) is lower than direct sales.
